## Executive Summary

A review of the AI Weekly Digest codebase, database schema, API routes, and frontend pages reveals that **no Terms of Service document or page currently exists**. The footer links to `/privacy` and `/about` but does not include a `/terms` link, and no `docs/terms*` file was found in the repository. This is the single most critical legal gap in the project.

The service collects subscriber email addresses via a subscribe form, stores them in a Supabase-hosted PostgreSQL database, and sends a weekly AI-curated newsletter. The homepage states "Free forever" and "No spam. Unsubscribe at any time." While these are positive signals, they are **insufficient substitutes for a binding Terms of Service agreement**.

**Overall Risk Rating: 🔴 Critical**
Five critical/high findings require immediate attention before a public launch.

## Detailed Findings

### Finding 1 — No Terms of Service Document Exists
**Severity: 🔴 Critical**

**Evidence:** No `/terms` route exists in `app/`, no `docs/terms*` file exists, and the Footer component (`components/layout/Footer.tsx`) does not include a Terms link. The only legal-adjacent page linked is `/privacy`.

**Risk:** Without a ToS, the service has no enforceable user agreement. There is no limitation of liability, no IP assignment, no acceptable-use policy, and no dispute resolution mechanism. This exposes the operator to significant legal liability, particularly in the EU (GDPR Article 13 requires information about legal basis for processing) and the United States.

**Remediation:** Create a Terms of Service page at `app/terms/page.tsx` and add a `/terms` link to the Footer. The ToS must be displayed and accepted (or at minimum, linked) before a user submits their email. Minimum required sections:

- Acceptance of Terms
- Description of Service
- User Obligations and Acceptable Use
- Intellectual Property
- Disclaimer of Warranties
- Limitation of Liability
- Termination
- Governing Law and Dispute Resolution
- Changes to Terms
- Contact Information

### Finding 2 — No Explicit ToS Acceptance at Subscription
**Severity: 🔴 Critical**

**Evidence:** The `SubscribeForm` component submits an email to `POST /api/subscribe`. The homepage reads "No spam. Unsubscribe at any time." but there is no checkbox, acknowledgement link, or consent capture tying the submission to acceptance of Terms of Service or Privacy Policy.

**Risk:** Under GDPR (applicable when serving EU residents), subscribing to a mailing list requires a lawful basis. Using "consent" as the lawful basis (common for newsletters) requires that consent be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous — implied consent mechanisms do not suffice. Under CAN-SPAM (US), affirmative consent is not strictly required for commercial email; however, a ToS acceptance protects the operator.

**Remediation — Suggested UI Language (below the subscribe button):**

```
By subscribing, you agree to our [Terms of Service] and [Privacy Policy].
You can unsubscribe at any time.
```

Link both `[Terms of Service]` and `[Privacy Policy]` to their respective pages. A stronger option is a checkbox:

```
☐ I agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy (required)
```

### Finding 3 — No Governing Law or Jurisdiction
**Severity: 🔴 Critical**

**Evidence:** No governing law clause exists anywhere in the codebase, documentation, or frontend pages.

**Risk:** Without a governing law clause, any dispute over the service defaults to whichever jurisdiction a plaintiff chooses to file in. This is especially problematic for international services.

**Remediation — Suggested Language:**

```
These Terms are governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of
[COUNTRY / STATE], without regard to its conflict of law principles.
You agree to submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts located
in [CITY, COUNTRY / STATE] to resolve any dispute arising out of these Terms.
```

Replace bracketed placeholders with the operator's actual jurisdiction.

### Finding 4 — No Age Restrictions / COPPA Compliance
**Severity: 🟠 High**

**Evidence:** The subscribe form has no age gate, minimum age declaration, or COPPA disclosure. The `subscribers` table schema stores no date-of-birth or age-verification field.

**Risk:** COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) applies to US operators collecting personal information (including email addresses) from children under 13. GDPR Article 8 sets the digital consent age at 16 (member states may lower to 13). Without an age restriction, the service may inadvertently collect data from minors, creating regulatory liability.

**Remediation:** Add a minimum age clause to the ToS and a notice at the subscription form:

```
This service is intended for users aged 16 and older (or 13+ in jurisdictions
where a lower age applies). By subscribing, you confirm that you meet the
minimum age requirement.
```

If children under 13 are not a target audience, state this explicitly in the ToS and consider adding a year-of-birth field to the subscription form as a lightweight gate.

### Finding 5 — Intellectual Property Risk: AI-Curated Summaries
**Severity: 🟠 High**

**Evidence:** The service curates AI news by republishing summaries of third-party articles (see `digest_items` table: `title`, `url`, `source`, `summary` fields). The `digest_items.summary` field (up to 1,000 characters) likely contains paraphrased or AI-generated summaries of third-party content.

**Risk:**
- **Copyright infringement**: Reproducing substantial portions of third-party articles, even in summary form, may constitute infringement under reproduction and derivative work rights. While brief summaries with attribution are generally protected under fair use (US) / fair dealing (UK/EU), the threshold is fact-specific.
- **AI-generated content**: If AI tools are used to generate summaries, the output may reproduce training-data content verbatim in edge cases.
- **Trade mark**: Reproducing publication names as sources is generally acceptable, but should not imply endorsement or affiliation.

**Remediation — Suggested ToS Language:**

```
Intellectual Property of Third Parties. AI Weekly Digest links to and
provides brief editorial summaries of publicly available third-party
content. All original content, trademarks, and intellectual property
rights remain with their respective owners. Our summaries constitute
fair use/fair dealing commentary and are not a substitute for the original
source. If you are a content owner and believe your rights have been
infringed, please contact us at [contact email].
```

Also add a DMCA / copyright takedown contact address.

### Finding 6 — CAN-SPAM / GDPR: No Confirmed Unsubscribe Mechanism in Emails
**Severity: 🟠 High**

**Evidence:** The `subscribers` table has an `unsubscribed_at` field and `DELETE /api/subscribe` exists server-side. However:
- No email templates are visible in the frontend codebase.
- There is no `/unsubscribe` page in `app/`.
- The `confirmed` column defaults to `false` with no visible confirmation email flow in `lib/services/subscriber.service.ts`.

**Risk:**
- **CAN-SPAM (US)**: Every commercial email must include a functional unsubscribe mechanism honoured within 10 business days. Absence of an unsubscribe link in emails violates this requirement (penalty: up to $53,088 per email).
- **GDPR**: Email marketing requires clear, easy withdrawal of consent. "Unsubscribe at any time" on the homepage is insufficient — every email must contain a one-click unsubscribe link.

**Remediation:**
1. Add a `/unsubscribe?email=[encoded_email]&token=[hmac_token]` route.
2. Include a one-click unsubscribe link in every outgoing email template.
3. Implement the double opt-in flow: on `POST /api/subscribe`, send a confirmation email; only set `confirmed = true` after the user clicks the link.

### Finding 7 — No Disclaimer of Liability for Content Accuracy
**Severity: 🟠 High**

**Evidence:** The service describes itself as curating "the most impactful developments in artificial intelligence." There are no disclaimers on the site disclaiming liability for the accuracy, completeness, or timeliness of curated content.

**Risk:** If a subscriber makes a business or investment decision based on inaccurate or outdated information in the digest, the operator could face negligence claims without adequate disclaimers. AI-generated summaries increase this risk as they may introduce errors or hallucinations.

**Remediation — Suggested ToS Language:**

```
Disclaimer of Warranties. The Service and all content are provided "as is"
and "as available" without warranties of any kind, express or implied.
AI Weekly Digest does not warrant the accuracy, completeness, reliability,
or timeliness of any information in the digest. Content is for informational
purposes only and does not constitute financial, investment, legal, or
professional advice. You rely on the Service at your own risk.

Limitation of Liability. To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law,
AI Weekly Digest and its operators shall not be liable for any indirect,
incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages arising out of or
in connection with your use of the Service or reliance on its content,
even if advised of the possibility of such damages.
```

### Finding 8 — No Data Retention Policy
**Severity: 🟡 Medium**

**Evidence:** The `subscribers` table retains records. The current service code calls `.delete()` on unsubscription (hard delete), not a soft update of `unsubscribed_at`. No retention schedule is defined anywhere.

**Risk:** GDPR Article 5(1)(e) requires personal data be kept no longer than necessary ("storage limitation"). Retaining unsubscribed email addresses indefinitely violates this principle.

**Remediation:**
- Define a data retention schedule: e.g., active subscriber data retained until unsubscription + 30 days; hashed suppression list retained up to 2 years to prevent accidental re-subscription.
- Document this in both the Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.
- Change the unsubscribe flow to a **soft delete** (set `unsubscribed_at`, keep row for suppression) and purge after the stated retention window.

### Finding 9 — Double Opt-In / Consent Record Not Implemented
**Severity: 🟡 Medium**

**Evidence:** `subscriber.service.ts` inserts rows with `confirmed: false` but there is no confirmation email dispatch, no confirmation token column in the schema, and no `/confirm` route in `app/`.

**Risk:** Without double opt-in, the operator cannot prove consent was given by the actual owner of an email address. Under GDPR, the controller must be able to demonstrate that the data subject gave consent (Article 7(1)). Lack of a consent record is an enforcement risk.

**Remediation:**
1. Add a `confirmation_token` column and `confirmation_sent_at` to the `subscribers` table.
2. On subscribe, send a confirmation email with a tokenised link.
3. Create `POST /api/confirm` to set `confirmed = true` and clear the token.
4. Only send digest emails to `confirmed = true` subscribers.

### Finding 10 — No Service Modification or Termination Clause
**Severity: 🟡 Medium**

**Evidence:** No documentation states the operator's rights to modify, suspend, or discontinue the service, nor what happens to subscriber data upon termination.

**Risk:** Without this clause, users could argue they have an ongoing entitlement to the service. Consumer protection laws in some jurisdictions (e.g., EU Consumer Rights Directive) require advance notice of material changes.

**Remediation — Suggested Language:**

```
Changes to Service. We reserve the right to modify, suspend, or discontinue
the Service at any time with reasonable notice (at least 30 days for material
changes). We will notify subscribers via email before discontinuation.
Upon termination of the Service, subscriber data will be deleted within
90 days in accordance with our Privacy Policy.
```

### Finding 11 — No Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for Supabase
**Severity: 🟡 Medium**

**Evidence:** The project uses Supabase (`@supabase/supabase-js`, `@supabase/ssr`) as the database and authentication provider. Subscriber email addresses are stored in Supabase-hosted infrastructure.

**Risk:** Under GDPR Article 28, any processor that handles personal data on behalf of a controller must have a Data Processing Agreement in place. Supabase offers a DPA, but the operator must actively execute it. Absent a DPA, the operator is in breach of GDPR.

**Remediation:**
1. Execute Supabase's DPA (available at supabase.com/legal/dpa) under your Supabase account.
2. Reference the DPA in your Privacy Policy as a third-party processor disclosure.
3. Review Supabase's sub-processor list and ensure users are informed of sub-processors in the Privacy Policy.

### Finding 12 — EU AI Act: No Disclosure of AI Use in Curation
**Severity: 🟢 Low**

**Evidence:** The Product Hunt listing and service description indicate AI tools may be used to curate or summarise content. No disclosure exists on the site or in any legal document.

**Risk:** Under the EU AI Act (in enforcement as of 2026), providers of AI systems must provide transparency about AI involvement in content generation or curation, particularly when content influences user decisions. The service's risk classification is likely "Minimal Risk" (informational newsletter), but transparency obligations still apply.

**Remediation — Suggested Disclosure in ToS and/or About page:**

```
AI-Assisted Curation. Some or all content summaries in AI Weekly Digest
may be generated or assisted by artificial intelligence tools. All content
is reviewed by human editors before publication. We do not use AI to make
automated decisions about individual users.
```

### Finding 13 — Auto-Renewal Disclosures
**Severity: 🟢 Low**

**Evidence:** The homepage states "Free forever." There is no billing, subscription tier, or payment integration in the current codebase.

**Risk:** Currently low. However, if a paid tier is introduced in the future (common for newsletters), auto-renewal disclosure requirements under California's Automatic Renewal Law (ARL), the FTC's Click-to-Cancel rule (effective 2026), and EU Consumer Rights Directive will apply.

**Remediation (Pre-emptive):** Before launching any paid tier, ensure:
- Clear disclosure of the charge amount, billing frequency, and auto-renewal before purchase.
- An equally prominent cancellation mechanism ("Click-to-Cancel").
- A cancellation confirmation email.
