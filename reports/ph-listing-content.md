## Maker First Comment

Hey Product Hunt 👋

I'm the solo founder behind **AI Weekly Digest**, and I want to be honest with you about why this thing exists.

It started as a personal problem. I was spending 3–4 hours every weekend trying to catch up on AI — wading through Twitter threads, Reddit posts, Hacker News discussions, research paper summaries, and tool launches. By the time I finished, I couldn't tell which 10% of it actually mattered. Sound familiar? I was exhausted before my week even started.

So I built the thing I wished existed: a weekly digest that does the filtering for me. Not another aggregator that dumps 50 links in your inbox. Not a newsletter written by a media company optimizing for ad clicks. Just the handful of AI developments that week that a builder — a founder, an indie dev, a product person — actually needs to know about. Each item gets context: *why does this matter, what does it change, what should you do with this information?* That's the whole product.

What makes it different? Honestly, it's the curation philosophy. I'm not trying to be comprehensive. I'm trying to be *useful*. Every edition goes through a simple filter: "If a smart indie founder had 10 minutes this week, is this worth one of those minutes?" Most things don't pass. The ones that do make it into the digest. I also write in plain language — no academic hedging, no hype, no "this changes everything" hot takes without the receipts to back them up.

I'm launching here because I want real feedback from builders who have strong opinions about signal vs. noise. Does the format work? Is the curation tight enough? What's missing? I'd genuinely love to hear what you think — the good, the bad, and the "why didn't you include X?" Drop a comment and I'll personally respond to every one. Thanks for checking it out. 🙏
